Current-partners: Pinydiit Foundation collaborates with several organizations in its efforts to improve education for young people in South Sudan. These partnerships provide the foundation with the necessary resources and expertise to achieve its mission of empowering underserved students through education. Through these collaborations, Pinydiit Foundation works closely with organizations that support refugees in the country and those that focus on education and community development in South Sudan. Together, these partnerships enable Pinydiit Foundation to identify and support students in need and provide them with school stationeries and scholarships.

Active Members:  Pinydiit Foundation's active members are a dedicated group of volunteers who are committed to the cause and share the belief that education is a fundamental right that should be accessible to all. The active members of the Pinydiit Foundation are composed of individuals from diverse backgrounds, including educators, community leaders, and individuals with a passion for social justice. They work tirelessly to identify and support students who face financial and social barriers to education. Through their efforts, the foundation is able to provide essential school supplies, such as exercise books, pens, pencils and erasers to students in need.

Milestone Achieved : While the organization is still in its early stages, it has already achieved two significant milestones: a) Its launch marks the beginning of its journey to empower young people through education. b) Founding of the organization- The organization was initiated by a dedicated team of individuals who share a passion for improving the lives of underserved students in the region. As the foundation continues to grow and expand its reach, it remains committed to providing access to education and supporting the academic pursuits of young people in need.

Awards : The organization is still in its early stages of development and has not yet received any awards for its work in the community. Who We Are

PINYDIIT FOUNDATION, INC- (pronouns as "Penydiet or Peny-hee-diet") is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization founded in January 2023 with the mission of providing school stationeries and scholarships to underserved students of internally displaced persons (IDPs) and refugee communities of South Sudanese origin within South Sudan and other East African countries. The foundation was named after the Awulian community's village in Nyuak Payam, Twic East County of Jonglei state, South Sudan. According to oral traditional history passed down on many generations, as time goes on, the Pinydiit village of Awulian became "Penydiet," a pronunciation deviation from the word "Pinydiit." Geographically, the village is located south of Bai Pawuoi, another Awulian village, and west of Pawuoi Wut (Awulain's cattle camp) in the Awulian territory. This information is based on the map of South Sudan's ten states, counties, and Payams, including Buma's boundaries. Buma formed the nucleus of government administrations in the country (Here, the description is only for informational purposes.)

Mission

Our mission is to provide school stationaries and scholarships to underserved students of internally displaced persons (IDPs) and refugee communities in South Sudan and other East African countries. In developing countries, wars disrupt youths' education, affecting future generations. This problem is even more daunting in a new country like South Sudan. The wars and internal conflicts disadvantage children of South Sudanese access to primary education. As wars forced communities to live in displaced and refugee camps for safety, children of South Sudan need/lack many items required for proper education, such as school supplies. In the developed world (e.g., the USA), school supplies like pencils, pens, and exercise books are taken for guaranteed. However, in the mentioned-above places (new and war-torn countries), these items are rare commodities that get in the way and strip kids of learning—disadvantaging generations of society.

Vision

Our vision is to fill the gap for underserved and disadvantaged students of IDPs and refugee communities by supplying essential school items that facilitate their schooling and toward building a better society (whole society).
