Current-partners Pinyindiit Foundation currently partners with three organizations in their efforts to improve education for South Sudanese youth. The first partner is the UNHCR, which provides support for the refugees in the country. The second partner is the South Sudanese Ministry of Education, which works closely with the foundation to identify and support students in need. The third partner is a local non-profit organization that focuses on education and community development in South Sudan. Together, these partners provide the necessary resources and expertise to help Pinyindiit Foundation achieve its mission of empowering young people through education.

Active Members Pinydiit Foundation's active members are a dedicated group of volunteers who are committed to the cause and share the belief that education is a fundamental right that should be accessible to all. The active members of the Pinyindiit Foundation are composed of individuals from diverse backgrounds, including educators, community leaders, and individuals with a passion for social justice. They work tirelessly to identify and support students who face financial and social barriers to education. Through their efforts, the foundation has been able to provide essential school supplies, such as books, pens, and backpacks, to students in need. Additionally, they have provided scholarships to cover tuition fees, transportation costs, and other expenses that can prevent students from attending school.

Milestone Achieved Pinydiit Foundation is committed to promoting education in the region and empowering young people to achieve their full potential. Over the years, Pinyindiit Foundation has achieved significant milestones in its mission to support the education of underserved students in South Sudan. One of its key achievements has been the distribution of school supplies, such as notebooks, pens, and pencils, to thousands of students in IDP and refugee communities. The organization has also awarded scholarships to many deserving students, enabling them to access quality education and pursue their dreams. Through these scholarships, Pinyindiit Foundation has helped to alleviate the financial burden on families who struggle to pay for their children's education. Furthermore, the organization has established partnerships with local schools and community organizations to support educational initiatives and promote access to education in the region. These partnerships have helped to create a network of support for underserved students and provided a platform for the organization to reach more young people in need.

Awards The Pinyindiit Foundation has been recognized for its outstanding work in the community, receiving several awards for its efforts. These awards include recognition for its innovative approach to education, its impact on the lives of children and families, and its dedication to improving the educational landscape for vulnerable communities in South Sudan. Through its initiatives, the Pinyindiit Foundation has helped to break down the barriers to education that many students face, providing them with the resources and support they need to succeed. The foundation's awards serve as a testament to the vital work it does and the impact it has on the lives of students in need.

Who We Are

PINYDIIT FOUNDATION (PF) is a 501 (c) (3) Nonprofit Organization founded in January 2023, whose mission is to provide school stationeries and scholarships to underserved students of internally displaced persons (IDPS) and refugee communities of Sudanese within South Sudan and in other East Africa countries.

Mission

In developing countries, wars disrupt youths' education, affecting future generations. This problem is even more daunting in a new country such as South Sudan. Sudanese (South) children are disadvantaged by the wars and internal conflicts that hide their access to primary education. Forced to live in displaced and refugee camps, children of South Sudan need/lack many items required for proper education, such as school supplies. PINYDIIT FOUNDATION's mission is to provide school stationaries and scholarships to underserved students of internally displaced persons (IDP) and refugee communities of South Sudan within South Sudan and other East African countries. In the developed world (e.g., USA), school supplies such as pencils, pens, and exercise books are taken for guarantee. However, in the mentioned-above places (new and wars-torn countries), these items are rare commodities that impair children's schooling and learning and disadvantage the next generations of society.

Vision

Our vision is to fill the gap for underserved and disadvantaged students of IDP and refugee communities by supplying essential school items that facilitate their schooling and toward building a better society (whole society).
